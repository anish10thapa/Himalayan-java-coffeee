The Journey

The Himalayan Java story

In 1999, when coffee culture barely existed in Nepal, Gagan Pradhan and Anand Gurung opened a small café in Kathmandu with a bold vision: give Nepalis access to world-class coffee made from their own Himalayan beans.

Inspired by the independent coffee shop culture he encountered while studying hospitality management in Sydney, Australia, Pradhan saw what Nepal's Arabica beans could become. Nepal's unique altitude, climate, and organic soil conditions produce a coffee of extraordinary character — chocolatey, floral, with bright citrus notes that rival the world's finest.

Their first sale? A banana smoothie. The early days were challenging — Nepalis weren't yet ready for the "bitter" beverage. But slowly, methodically, Himalayan Java built a culture. Today, Kathmandu alone has over 4,000 cafés — a movement the brand helped ignite.

We are proud to bring that legacy to Itahari — Eastern Nepal's growing, ambitious city. Our Itahari branch carries the same commitment: 100% Nepali Arabica, expert baristas, warm hospitality, and a space where ideas, stories, and great coffee all live together.

🌱 Ilam Coffee Farm, Eastern Nepal
Origin

From the Himalayan foothills

Nepal's coffee-growing regions — Ilam, Nuwakot, Sindhupalchok, and Palpa — sit between 1,000 and 2,000 metres above sea level, in the shadow of the world's tallest mountains.

The cool misty air, rich volcanic soil, and careful organic farming practices produce Arabica beans of exceptional complexity. Himalayan Java operates its own farm in Ilam — giving us direct control from harvest to roast.

Every cup you drink in our Itahari café was grown, processed, roasted, and crafted entirely in Nepal. That's a story worth tasting.
